Human Uses


Many activities can be done in the tundra. There are many activities such as husky racing, wildlife viewing, seal hunting, and even scientific expeditions.

Husky racing

This is a very popular sport in Alaska. They hook up a team of Husky dogs to a sledge and race through the snow. See more at http://www.enchantedlearning.com/usa/states/alaska/iditarod/

Wildlife Watching

Many people in Canada like going out with binoculars and other such equipment to see the native wildlife. See more at http://www.thelon.com/wolves.htm

Scientific & drilling stations

There are many scientific stations in the Arctic Tundra dedicated to recording the rate of which the ice is melting or to observe the wildlife. There are also many drilling stations in the sea near the arctic coast. These stations drill deep down into the earth's crust, looking for any oil or such fossil fuels. See more at http://www.arctic.ac.uk/infrastructure/international-facilities/international-stations
