Animal Adaptation


Many biomes have animals adapted to them. The tundra is no exeption. Many of the animals here have adapted to survive in harsh snowstorms and cold, cold nights. These adaptations not only help animals, they are cruitial to their survival. Without some of these special adaptations, these animals would have died out long ago!

Other Animals

  • The polar bear has a thick coat of transparent fur that reflects light(this is why it appears white), under this coat it also has black skin which absorbs heat.

  • The arctic hare has long claws used for digging and has thickly padded soles to help it withstand the cold. It has a thick white coat to blend in and keep warm. when winter approaches the hare's coat grows longer and thicker. They form large packs so that some act as guards while others rest

Arctic Fox

The Arctic Fox has a white coat to blend in with it's surroundings. It also has really thich fur, this is to help it better keep warm.

Snowy Owl

The Snowy Owl has a pure white plummage to help it blend in. This is to hide from predators. It's head, like all owles is adapted so it can spin 360 degrees.

Harp Seal

A harp seal has extrordinarily thick blubber to help keep itself warm in the freezing depths of the ocean.
