Threats


Humans have overall been quite an negative impact on the tundra. They are digging into the grounds and breaking habitat for oil deep in the ground. Humans have broken the habitats of many native species by mining for zinc, aluminum, and gold. The problems from humans are pollution, poaching, and global warming. Animals are being poisoned by pollution in the sea. There is also poaching, the hunting of animal species for skins, for example the Polar Bears are almost extinct. Global Warming is a worldwide increase in temperature, which have been caused by human emitted greenhouse gases. If it keeps going, all the snow in the the ice caps, tundras and glaciers will melt. If this happens, many low lying cities and islands will become completly submerged. Many species native to these places will go extinct!

Polar Bears are almost extinct!

  • The polar bears, once at the top of the food chain, are now nearly extinct. As icebergs and glaciers melt, these mighty predators are starving and don't have anywhere to go. The snow where they once found shelter in are no longer safe, the icebergs that they had their dens during the winter no longer exist. Soon the only polar bears left may be the ones in captivity.
